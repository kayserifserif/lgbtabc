LGBTFAQ

A terminology board of LGBT+ terms, created by the CIS GSA (Gender and Sexuality Alliance).

Sexual attraction
Demiromanticism
Feels romantic attraction only after forming a deep connection with another individual.
Straight
Attracted to people of the opposite sex.
Pansexual
Attracted to all genders.
Ally
Solidarity with a public cause.
Agender
‘Without’ gender. A person who does not identify with any particular gender, male or female.
Bi-curious
People of a heterosexual or homosexual identity who, while showing some curiosity for sexual activity with a person of the sex they do not favor, distinguish themselves from the bisexual label.
Panromanticism
Romantic attraction to all genders.
Closeted
LGBTQ etc. people who have not disclosed their sexual orientation or gender identity and aspects thereof.
Butch
Masculine gender expression (typically refers to masculine women).
Transsexual
A person who identifies psychologically as a gender different to that assigned at birth. People who are transexual often use hormonal and or surgical methods to transform their gender.
Heterosexual
Sexually attracted to the opposite sex.
Trans
Umbrella term for all genders within the gender identity spectrum.
Homosexual
Attracted to the same gender.
Gender identity
A person's learnt understanding and alignment of ‘labels’ (male/female/trans*).
Androgynous
A type of gender expression neither exclusively masculine or feminine.
Gender expression
A person's behaviour, mannerisms, interests, and appearance in association with gender.
Gay
A person who is attracted to people of the same gender / a man who is attracted to men.
Heteronormative
The assumption, in individuals or in institutions, that everyone is heterosexual, and that heterosexual identities are superior to other identities or people.
Genderqueer / genderfluid
Not conforming to binary gender identities.
Femme
Feminine gender expression (typically refers to feminine men).
Intersex
Person with sexual anatomy not conforming to traditional male and female biology.
Transphobia
Prejudice against transgender people.
Dyke
Derogatory slur for lesbians.
Advocate
A person who actively supports a public cause.
Hermaphrodite
Person with male and female reproductive organs.
Homophobia
Prejudice against homosexuality.
Transgender
A person who identifies as the gender other than assigned to them at birth.
Aromanticism
Experiencing little or no romantic attraction to others and/or has a lack of interest in romantic relationships/behavior.
Bisexual
Attracted to both the same and opposite genders/sexes.
Sissy
Slur for feminine gender expression.
Queer
Umbrella term for all people who are not traditionally heterosexual and/or cisgender.
Asexual
Does not feel sexual attraction to any gender.
Outing
When someone reveals information about another person's sexual orientation and/or gender identity without their consent.
Cisgender
Denoting or relating to a person whose self-identity conforms with the gender that corresponds to their biological sex; not transgender.
Lesbian
A woman who is sexually and romantically attracted to other women.
Biological sex
The label that is assigned at birth which is based on one's chromosomes, hormones and sexual organs.
Faggot
Offensive slur for gay men.
Non-binary
Someone who doesn't identify as either female or male.
Cisnormativity
The assumption, in individuals or in institutions, that everyone is cisgender, and that cisgender identities are superior to trans* identities or people.
Kinsey scale
A scale that is used to quantify a person's sexual orientation from on scale of 1–5, 1 being completely straight, 5 being completely gay.
